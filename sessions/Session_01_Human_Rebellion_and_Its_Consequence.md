# Session 1
## Human Rebellion and Its Consequence (Sin + Death)

### How to Use This Page
- Everyone: read the Scripture, answer the questions, and write personal responses.
- Teacher: use the `Guide Note` lines to keep discussion moving.
- Learner: use the `Personal Note` lines to reflect and respond.

### Big Idea
Sin is universal rebellion against God, and death is its consequence; therefore, humanity needs rescue, not self-repair.

### Learning Goal
By the end of this session, we should be able to explain:
- sin as both guilt before God and a ruling power in human life
- death as the consequence of sin's reign, not only a biological event
- why this makes the gospel necessary

### Key Terms
- `Sin`: rebellion against God in thought, desire, and action
- `Death`: the consequence of sin, including alienation from God and final judgment apart from Christ
- `Sins` and `Sin`: individual acts and ruling power
- `Weakness` and `Guilt`: humanity is not only weak; humanity is morally accountable

### Summary
Paul teaches that all are under sin, all are accountable to God, and death is sin's wage. This truth removes boasting and prepares us for grace.

### Response
- Confess sin honestly instead of minimizing it.
- Reject self-justification and comparison.
- Grow compassion, because all people share this need.

### Memory Anchor

- "Where sin reigns, death follows."
