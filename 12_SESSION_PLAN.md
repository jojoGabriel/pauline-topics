## Session Template (Use Every Week)

1. Opening and prayer (5 min)
2. Big question for the night (5 min)
3. Read key passages aloud (15 min)
4. Observation discussion: "What does Paul say?" (20 min)
5. Meaning discussion: "How do these passages connect?" (20 min)
6. Response discussion: "What should change in us?" (10 min)
7. Weekly takeaway and closing prayer (5 min)

## Session-by-Session Outline

1. Human Rebellion and Its Consequence (Sin + Death)
   - Big question: What has gone wrong with humanity?
   - Aim: See sin as both guilt and power, and death as its outcome.
   - Takeaway: We need rescue, not self-improvement.

2. Guilt Before God and True Righteousness (Condemnation + Righteousness)
   - Big question: Why can't human righteousness solve our problem?
   - Aim: Understand condemnation and God's righteous standard.
   - Takeaway: Our need is moral and legal before God.

3. The Good News of Christ (The Gospel + The Cross)
   - Big question: What is the gospel, and why is the cross central?
   - Aim: Define the gospel from Paul and center it on Christ's work.
   - Takeaway: Christianity stands on what Christ did.

4. What Christ Accomplished (Redemption + Reconciliation + Atonement)
   - Big question: What exactly did Jesus accomplish for sinners?
   - Aim: Distinguish and connect these three salvation terms.
   - Takeaway: The cross solves guilt, alienation, and judgment.

5. Why God Saves (Mercy + Love of God)
   - Big question: What moved God toward sinners?
   - Aim: Ground salvation in God's character, not our worth.
   - Takeaway: Salvation flows from divine mercy and love.

6. Salvation Given and Received (Salvation + Grace + Faith)
   - Big question: How is salvation received?
   - Aim: Show grace as source and faith as means.
   - Takeaway: Salvation is gift, received by trust.

7. Justified Before God (Justification by Faith)
   - Big question: What does it mean to be justified?
   - Aim: Clarify legal standing, peace, and assurance in Christ.
   - Takeaway: Believers stand accepted before God.

8. Immediate Blessings of Salvation (Peace with God + Access to God + Forgiveness)
   - Big question: What changes immediately when we are saved?
   - Aim: Trace relational and experiential results of justification.
   - Takeaway: We are no longer estranged from God.

9. Life That Cannot Be Lost (Eternal Life)
   - Big question: What does eternal life mean now and forever?
   - Aim: Present eternal life as present possession and future hope.
   - Takeaway: New life begins now and endures forever.

10. God's Purpose in Salvation (Calling + Election)
   - Big question: How do God's calling and choosing shape assurance?
   - Aim: Emphasize humility, gratitude, and confidence in God's purpose.
   - Takeaway: Salvation rests finally in God's initiative.

11. The Believer's New Position (Union with Christ)
   - Big question: What does it mean to be "in Christ"?
   - Aim: Show participation in Christ's death and resurrection life.
   - Takeaway: Identity and growth come from union with Jesus.

12. The Believer's New Identity (New Creation)
   - Big question: What kind of people are we now?
   - Aim: Integrate new identity, new life, and new way of walking.
   - Takeaway: The gospel creates a new people with a new life direction.
