# Death (as consequence of sin)
**Paul’s Teachings by Topic**  
**Volume 1: Salvation and Foundations**  
**A Structured Topical Study from the Letters of Paul the Apostle Using Selected Passages**  
**Text Basis:** Berean Standard Bible (BSB)

---

## 1. Definition

In this topic, death is the consequence that follows human rebellion against God.  
In Paul’s letters, it appears as the result of Adam’s trespass and stands in contrast to the life God gives in Christ.

---

## 7. Summary

Paul teaches that death entered the world through sin and came to all because all sinned.  
Death reigned through Adam’s trespass and stands as sin’s wage.  
Yet God answers the reign of death through Jesus Christ, bringing life where sin had ruled.  
Believers are therefore to regard themselves as brought from death to life.

## 10. Reflection Questions

1. Why is it important that Paul connects death directly to sin rather than treating it as morally neutral?
2. How does [Romans 6:23](https://biblehub.com/bsb/romans/6.htm) sharpen the contrast between what sin pays and what God gives?
3. What stands out to you about Paul’s claim in [Romans 6:13](https://biblehub.com/bsb/romans/6.htm) that believers have been brought from death to life?

## 11. Application Questions

1. How should Paul’s teaching about death as the wage of sin shape the seriousness with which you view sin?
